INCIDENT REPORT: DIGITAL FRAUD CASE

Under Active Investigation & Prioritized for Action

Case ID: DF-2025-PH-001

Issued by: Digital Forensics Unit

Date of Report: July 28, 2025

Overview of Fraudulent Activity

This report details a meticulously investigated online fraud incident resulting in a financial loss amounting to PHP 17,633. The Digital Forensics Unit has compiled preliminary evidence of deceptive practices and malicious retaliation, and this case is now **under active investigation and being prioritized** for comprehensive resolution.

Initial Deception and False Legitimacy

The fraudulent scheme originated with a Telegram account, identified as 'Valoria Marine,' falsely claiming affiliation with 'Robinsonâ€™s Mall Corporation.' The victim was subsequently directed to a deceptive website, https://unemployedph.store, which presented itself as a legitimate online part-time job platform offering commission-based tasks through e-commerce product purchases. Initial engagement involved a PHP 100 registration fee. A small 'welcome profit' of PHP 15 was disbursed, followed by a successful withdrawal of PHP 144 from a minor task, designed to establish a false sense of legitimacy and trust.

Escalation of Financial Demands

Encouraged by the initial success, the victim was prompted to 'top-up' PHP 300 for a subsequent task, with promised returns of PHP 510-600. Following this, a larger task involving the purchase of nine (9) products was assigned. The prices of these products progressively increased, necessitating continuous additional deposits from the victim: PHP 286, PHP 1,161, PHP 2,948, and a final substantial sum of PHP 12,938. The victim reported feeling compelled to continue investing to avoid losing already committed funds.

The Illegitimate 'Income Tax Return' Demand

Upon completion of all nine tasks, the victim initiated the withdrawal process. However, the individual identified as 'Valoria Marine' then demanded an 'Income Tax Return (ITR)' fee, to be paid personally by the victim to the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR), before any total earnings or deposited funds could be released. This procedure was immediately questioned by the victim, who correctly stated that tax deductions are typically handled by the company or organization. Despite the victim's objections, the perpetrator insisted on out-of-pocket payment and falsely advised visiting the BIR for verification. The perpetrator refused to return the invested PHP 17,633, claiming funds were 'already deposited into a bank account under their management' and were pending release, contingent upon the ITR fee payment.

Retaliation and False Accusations

In an attempt to resolve the escalating situation, the victim sought assistance from a government employee to communicate on their behalf. During this conversation, 'Valoria Marine' demanded proof of the government employee's identity, such as a valid ID, which was rightfully refused due to concerns of identity misuse. Subsequently, the perpetrator maliciously reported the victim's GCash account, falsely alleging impersonation of a BIR official, despite no such claim being made during the exchange.

Ongoing Investigation & Public Action

  • The Telegram account 'Valoria Marine' and associated entities are under active scrutiny as perpetrators of systematic online fraud.
  • The website https://unemployedph.store is confirmed as a fraudulent platform, and its operations are being actively monitored.
  • All collected evidence, including communication logs, transaction records, and digital footprints, is being meticulously analyzed for legal proceedings.
  • This case is being prioritized to ensure swift and comprehensive action against the perpetrators.
